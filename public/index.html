Who am I?

As a rising junior studying computer science and economics, I am interested in empowering people through behavior design and solving high-level problems. I love working in teams where I can wear many hats, adapting and using both technical and non-technical skills to tackle new challenges.

Outside of coursework, I lead as managing director of Helix (an undergraduate healthcare tech incubator), I play on the Yale Mens Club Volleyball Team, and I help coordinate student art exhibitions as Events Chair for Art Exhibition Students at Yale.

What's Keeping Me Busy Right Now

I'm currently working at ApolloMed as a software engineer intern, doing full-stack web app development with Flask, Python, HTML, CSS, JS, and SQL Server.

I also started volunteering for the data science team at GetUsPPE.org, the largest organization in the US helping coordinate PPE donations to frontline healthcare workers.

Additionally, I recently took EDUC 302 Behavior Design for Coronavirus Challenges, a course taught at Stanford by BJ Fogg. Because of the global nature of the course topic for this year, the class was open to non-Stanford students to apply. I worked on a variety of projects that harness the power of behavior design to solve some of the problems people are facing due to the pandemic. For example, my personal project utilized anchor behaviors in my own life to prompt me to call my grandparents every day and keep in touch. Here's another project I did to help couples in long distance relationships. My final project was QueuePals, a virtual waiting room solution to help patients safely check into their doctor's appointments over text message.
